About the journal

Africa Amani Journal

Published by IPSTC since 2021 · Quarterly · Open Access · Double-blind peer reviewed

Mandate & scope

The Africa Amani Journal (AAJ) is a peer-reviewed, open-access publication of the International Peace and Security Training Centre (IPSTC), Nairobi. It provides a rigorous academic platform for policy-relevant research on peace, security, and governance across Africa, with particular focus on the Horn of Africa and Indian Ocean region.

Publishing model

AAJ is fully open access — all articles are freely available online under Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 (CC-BY 4.0) licensing immediately upon publication. The journal employs rigorous double-blind peer review and adheres to COPE (Committee on Publication Ethics) guidelines. Submission is free; an Article Processing Charge (APC) of USD 150 applies only on acceptance, with waivers available for authors in low- or middle-income countries.

Publication standards

Policies & guidelines

Author guidelines

Manuscripts must be 6,000–10,000 words (excluding references), written in English, following Chicago Author-Date 18th edition. Files must be .docx, fully anonymised. Abstract: 250 words max. Keywords: 5–7.

Publication ethics

AAJ adheres to COPE guidelines on authorship, plagiarism, and research integrity. Submission implies the work is original, unpublished, and not under consideration elsewhere.

AI use policy In effect Nov 2025

Authors must disclose any use of AI writing or analysis tools in a dedicated disclosure section. AI tools may not be listed as co-authors under any circumstances.

Open access & copyright

All articles are published open access under Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 (CC-BY 4.0). Submission is free of charge. An Article Processing Charge (APC) of USD 150 is invoiced only upon acceptance of a manuscript. Authors affiliated with institutions in low- or middle-income countries (per World Bank classification) may apply for a full or partial waiver at the point of acceptance; waiver requests have no influence on editorial decisions.

Help centre

Frequently asked questions

How do I submit a manuscript?

Go to Submit Article in the nav or use your Dashboard. Fill in the manuscript details, upload your anonymised .docx file, complete the compliance checklist, and click Submit.

Is there a submission charge?

Submission is free. An Article Processing Charge (APC) of USD 150 applies only if your manuscript is accepted for publication. Authors at institutions in low- or middle-income countries may request a full or partial waiver at the point of acceptance — this has no bearing on the editorial decision.

What type of peer review does AAJ use?

AAJ uses double-blind peer review. Neither authors nor reviewers are identified to each other during the review process.

How long does the review process take?

The average time from submission to first decision is seven weeks. Peer review takes four to six weeks.

What citation style does AAJ use?

AAJ uses Chicago Author-Date, 18th edition.

What is AAJ's AI use policy?

Authors must disclose any use of AI writing or analysis tools. AI tools may not be listed as co-authors. The updated policy is in effect from November 2025.

Still have a question?

Our editorial team typically responds within two business days.

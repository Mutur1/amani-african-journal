Mandate & scope

The Amani African Journal (AAJ) is a peer-reviewed, open-access publication of the International Peace and Security Training Centre (IPSTC), Nairobi. It provides a rigorous academic platform for policy-relevant research on peace, security, and governance across Africa, with particular focus on the Horn of Africa and Indian Ocean region.

Aims & objectives

AAJ bridges the gap between academic research and policy practice, publishing original research articles, systematic reviews, and policy briefs that advance understanding of African peace and security dynamics. The journal actively encourages submissions from African scholars and practitioners, and prioritises research with direct implications for regional policymakers.

Publishing model

AAJ is fully open access — all articles are freely available online under Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 (CC-BY 4.0) licensing immediately upon publication. The journal employs rigorous double-blind peer review and adheres to COPE (Committee on Publication Ethics) guidelines. No article processing charges are levied at any stage.

Indexing & discoverability

AAJ is indexed in African Journals Online (AJOL), Google Scholar, and the Directory of Open Access Journals (ROAD). Applications to Scopus and DOAJ are in progress. All articles are assigned DOIs via Crossref for permanent, citable references.

How we work

Editorial process

AAJ follows a rigorous, transparent double-blind peer review process. Average time from submission to first decision is seven weeks.

Step-by-step workflow
1. Submission
Day 0
Author submits via portal. Submission ID auto-generated. Confirmation email dispatched immediately. Manuscript enters the system.
2. Initial screening
Within 5 business days
Editor checks scope, formatting, word count, plagiarism (Turnitin), and ethics compliance. Out-of-scope manuscripts desk-rejected with feedback.
3. Reviewer assignment
Days 5–10
Two independent reviewers identified and invited. Authors and reviewers are mutually anonymous throughout. Reviewers given 3-week deadline.
4. Double-blind peer review
4–6 weeks
Reviewers assess originality, methodological rigour, relevance, and clarity. Structured review form used. Deadline strictly enforced.
5. Editorial decision
Within 1 week of receiving reviews
Editor-in-Chief issues decision letter: Accept / Minor revisions / Major revisions / Reject. Reviewer comments shared with author.
6. Revision & resubmission
Author deadline set by editor
Author uploads revised manuscript and a point-by-point response to reviewers via the portal. Revision period typically 3–4 weeks.
7. Production & publication
2–3 weeks after final acceptance
Copyediting, author proofing, DOI assignment via Crossref, and online-first publication. Author notified automatically.

Peer review policy

AAJ uses double-blind peer review. Neither authors nor reviewers are identified to each other during the review process. All reviewer recommendations are advisory; the final decision rests with the Editor-in-Chief.

Reviewers are selected based on expertise in the manuscript's thematic area and methodological approach. The editorial team maintains a database of over 38 active reviewers across Africa and internationally.

Publication standards

Policies & guidelines

AAJ adheres to the highest standards of publication ethics in line with COPE guidelines and international best practice.

Author guidelines

Manuscripts must be 6,000–10,000 words (excluding references), written in English, following Chicago Author-Date 18th edition. Files must be .docx, fully anonymised. Abstract: 250 words max. Keywords: 5–7. All co-authors must be listed with affiliations and must have approved the final submission. Policy briefs: 2,000–4,000 words. Book reviews: 800–1,500 words.

Publication ethics

AAJ adheres to COPE guidelines on authorship, plagiarism, and research integrity. Authorship requires substantial intellectual contribution, drafting, and approval of the final manuscript. Submission implies the work is original, unpublished, and not under consideration elsewhere. Confirmed misconduct — including fabrication, falsification, or plagiarism — may result in retraction and reporting to the author's institution.

AI use policy In effect Nov 2025

Authors must disclose any use of AI writing or analysis tools (e.g. ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot) in a dedicated disclosure section within the manuscript. AI tools may not be listed as co-authors under any circumstances. Undisclosed AI use constitutes an ethics violation subject to the full misconduct procedure, including possible retraction.

Open access & copyright

All articles are published open access under Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 (CC-BY 4.0). Authors retain copyright and grant AAJ a perpetual, non-exclusive licence to publish. No article processing charges (APCs) are levied at any stage — submission, review, or publication are all free.

Data availability

Authors are strongly encouraged to make research data publicly available in a recognised repository. All submissions must include a data availability statement. Where restrictions apply — ethical, legal, or proprietary — authors must clearly state the reason. Data sharing statements are published alongside the article.

Conflict of interest

All authors must disclose financial, professional, or personal relationships that could be perceived to influence the research or its conclusions. Reviewers and editors must recuse themselves from manuscripts where any conflict of interest exists. Undisclosed conflicts discovered post-publication will result in a correction or retraction.

Plagiarism detection

All submissions are screened using Turnitin prior to peer review. Manuscripts with similarity scores exceeding the journal threshold are returned to authors without review, pending clarification or correction. Suspected plagiarism in published articles is investigated under the COPE guidelines on retraction.

Help centre

Frequently asked questions

Answers to common questions about submitting, the review process, policies, and publication. Can't find what you need? Contact us →

Submission process
How do I submit a manuscript?

Click Submit in the navigation or visit the Submit page. You will need to register for a free account first. Fill in the manuscript details, upload your anonymised .docx file, complete the compliance checklist, and click Submit. You will receive an automatic confirmation email with your submission ID.

What file format should I use?

Submit your manuscript as a .docx file (Microsoft Word). The file must be fully anonymised — remove your name, affiliation, and any identifying information from both the document content and file properties before uploading.

Can I submit to a special issue and the general issue simultaneously?

No. You may only submit your manuscript to one issue at a time. Select either the special issue or general submission during the submission process. If your manuscript is rejected from the special issue, you may resubmit to the general pool.

Is there an article processing charge (APC)?

No. AAJ is fully open access with no article processing charges. Submission, review, and publication are free for all authors. All published articles are freely available under CC-BY 4.0.

Can I submit a paper previously presented at a conference?

Yes, provided the conference paper has not been formally published in a proceedings journal or similar outlet. You must disclose in your cover letter that an earlier version was presented at a conference.

Peer review
What type of peer review does AAJ use?

AAJ uses double-blind peer review. Neither authors nor reviewers are identified to each other during the review process. All reviewer recommendations are advisory; the final decision rests with the Editor-in-Chief.

How long does the review process take?

The average time from submission to first decision is seven weeks. Initial screening takes up to five business days. Peer review takes four to six weeks. We aim to issue editorial decisions within one week of receiving reviewer reports.

How do I track my submission?

Log in to your author portal and visit the Track page. Enter your submission ID to see the current status, timeline, reviewer progress, and any messages from the editorial team. You also receive automatic email notifications at each stage.

What do the different decisions mean?

Accept — ready for production as submitted. Minor revisions — small changes required, no re-review needed. Major revisions — substantial changes required, revised version may be re-reviewed. Reject — the manuscript does not meet AAJ's publication standards at this time.

Formatting & style
What citation style does AAJ use?

AAJ uses Chicago Author-Date, 18th edition. References must be formatted consistently throughout the manuscript. A downloadable template and style guide are available on the Call for Papers page.

What is the word limit?

Research articles and review articles: 6,000–10,000 words excluding references. Policy briefs: 2,000–4,000 words. Book reviews: 800–1,500 words. Abstracts must not exceed 250 words.

How many keywords should I include?

Include five to seven keywords that accurately reflect the content of your manuscript. Avoid replicating words from the title. Keywords help readers find your article through search engines and indexing databases.

Ethics & policies
What is AAJ's AI use policy?

Authors must disclose any use of AI writing or analysis tools in a dedicated section of the manuscript. AI tools may not be listed as co-authors. Undisclosed AI use constitutes an ethics violation. The updated policy is in effect from November 2025.

How does AAJ check for plagiarism?

All submissions are screened using Turnitin before peer review. Manuscripts exceeding the journal's similarity threshold are returned to authors without review, pending clarification or correction.

Can I post my manuscript on a preprint server?

Yes. AAJ allows authors to post preprint versions of their manuscripts on recognised servers such as AfricArXiv or SSRN. You must inform the editorial team at submission and update the preprint record with the final DOI upon publication.

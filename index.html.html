About the journal

Amani African Journal

Published by IPSTC since 2021. Rigorously peer-reviewed, fully open access, policy-relevant.

Mandate & scope

The Amani African Journal (AAJ) is a peer-reviewed, open-access publication of the International Peace and Security Training Centre (IPSTC), Nairobi. It provides a rigorous academic platform for policy-relevant research on peace, security, and governance across Africa, with a focus on the Horn of Africa and Indian Ocean region.

Aims & objectives

AAJ bridges the gap between academic research and policy practice, publishing original research articles, systematic reviews, and policy briefs that advance understanding of African peace and security dynamics. The journal actively encourages submissions from African scholars and practitioners.

Publishing model

AAJ is fully open access — all articles are freely available under Creative Commons CC-BY 4.0 licensing. The journal employs rigorous double-blind peer review and adheres to COPE (Committee on Publication Ethics) guidelines. No article processing charges are levied.

How we work

Editorial process

AAJ follows a rigorous, transparent double-blind peer review process. The average time from submission to first decision is seven weeks.

1. Submission
Day 0
Author submits via portal. Auto-generated ID and confirmation email dispatched immediately.
2. Initial screening
Within 5 business days
Editor checks scope, formatting, plagiarism (Turnitin), and ethics compliance. Out-of-scope manuscripts are desk-rejected.
3. Reviewer assignment
Days 5–10
Two independent reviewers identified and invited. Authors and reviewers are mutually anonymous.
4. Double-blind peer review
4–6 weeks
Reviewers assess originality, methodological rigour, relevance to the field, and clarity of argument. Review deadline is strictly enforced.
5. Editorial decision
Within 1 week of receiving reviews
Editor-in-Chief issues a decision letter: Accept / Minor revisions / Major revisions / Reject. Reviewer comments shared with author.
6. Revision & resubmission
Author deadline set by editor
Author uploads revised version and a detailed response-to-reviewers document via the portal.
7. Production & publication
2–3 weeks after final acceptance
Copyediting, proofing, DOI assignment, and online-first publication. Author notified automatically.

Publication standards

Policies & guidelines

AAJ adheres to the highest standards of publication ethics, in line with COPE guidelines and international best practice.

Author guidelines

Manuscripts must be 6,000–10,000 words (excluding references), written in English, following Chicago Author-Date 18th edition. Files must be .docx, fully anonymised. Abstract: 250 words max. Keywords: 5–7. All co-authors must be listed with affiliations and must have approved the final submission.

Publication ethics

AAJ adheres to COPE guidelines on authorship, plagiarism, and research integrity. Authorship requires substantial intellectual contribution, drafting, and approval of the final manuscript. Submission implies the work is original, unpublished, and not under consideration elsewhere. Confirmed misconduct may result in retraction and reporting to the author's institution.

AI use policy (in effect November 2025)

Authors must disclose any use of AI writing or analysis tools (e.g. ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot) in a dedicated disclosure section within the manuscript. AI tools may not be listed as co-authors. Undisclosed AI use constitutes an ethics violation subject to the full misconduct procedure.

Open access & copyright

All articles are published open access under Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 (CC-BY 4.0). Authors retain copyright and grant AAJ a perpetual, non-exclusive licence to publish. No article processing charges (APCs) are levied.

Data availability

Authors are strongly encouraged to make research data publicly available. All submissions must include a data availability statement. Where restrictions apply (ethical, legal, or proprietary), authors must state the reason.

Conflict of interest

All authors must disclose financial, professional, or personal relationships that could be perceived to influence their work. Reviewers and editors must recuse themselves from manuscripts where any conflict exists.

Plagiarism detection

All submissions are screened using Turnitin prior to peer review. Manuscripts with similarity scores exceeding the journal threshold are returned to authors without review pending clarification or correction.
